Tundra!

You will find the most excilerating tundra animals here!

The tundra is the coldest of the biomes.It's dry - The tundra gets about as much precipitation as the average desert, around 10 inches per year. Permafrost - Below the top soil, the ground is permanently frozen year round.It's barren - The tundra has few nutrients to support plant and animal life.

White as snow, the snow hare. They have a life span of 18 months. Arctic Hares will dig holes under the snow or into the ground to both keep warm and to sleep. Artic Hares have black eyelashes that protect their eyes from glare. Because of their eye placement Arctic Hares can see 360 degrees around them without turning their heads. Their diet consist of woody plants, mosses, and lichens which they may dig through the snow to find in winter.

The musk ox. With such a big coat of fur it helps survive in the tundra. Musk oxen live about 12 to 20 years.Musk oxen are hunted by arctic wolves and occasionally polar bears.When threatened, musk oxen gather in a circle with their heads facing out.Their diet consist of plants, including grasses, sedges, forbs, and woody plants.
